# DTS '35 Ede Design System

> *Your football home in Ede, where everyone plays, grows, and belongs.*

This design system supports **V.V. DTS '35 Ede** (full name *Door Training Sterker '35*) — a Dutch amateur football club founded in **1935**, based at **Sportpark Peppelensteeg, Inschoterweg 2, 6715 CS Ede**, Gelderland, Netherlands. The club is volunteer‑driven, age‑inclusive (youth JO8–JO19 + MO girls' teams, women's VR1–VR3, men's senior 1–9, O23, 35+ leagues), and best known regionally for hosting an annual international **U15 Top Tournament** on Maandag 25 mei (Whit Monday) — currently in its 23rd edition.

The visual identity is built on a tight palette of two **blauw‑witte** (blue‑white) blues, a generous gray scale, a circular crest badge dated *19 35*, and a sober, clean grotesque sans‑serif. The tone is the voice of a *trusted neighbor*: factual, present‑tense, Dutch, never marketing‑slick.

## CONTENT FUNDAMENTALS

The club's voice is **direct, Dutch, present‑tense, and grounded** — the way you'd describe a Sunday match to a neighbor over the fence. It is never aspirational marketing‑speak; it never tries to be world‑class.

### Tone

- **Trusted neighbor, not corporate spokesperson.** The voice belongs to a club that has been part of this town since 1935. It does not need to prove itself.
- **Collective, not individual.** "DTS Ede heeft gewonnen", not "Our star player won." Match reports celebrate the team and the club, not single names.
- **Warm but not gushing.** Welcoming, but factual. Achievements are acknowledged without superlatives.
- **Honest about the ups and downs of amateur sport.** A 4‑0 loss gets the same straight reporting as a 4‑0 win.

### Casing & writing rules

- **Headlines:** Sentence case or Title Case for editorial pieces. Section banners (e.g. `WELKOM BIJ DTS EDE`) appear in **uppercase** as a deliberate civic / sign‑plaque feel — use sparingly.
- **Third person** for match reports and news: *"DTS Ede organiseert dit toernooi voor de 23e keer."* Avoid *"Wij organiseren..."* in editorial.
- **Active voice, short sentences.** Average 15–20 words. Front‑load the most important fact (result, date, action) in the first sentence.
- **Present tense for headlines** (`DTS Ede zoekt talent`), **past tense for match reports** (`DTS Ede heeft gewonnen`).
- **Dutch first**, English never appears in primary copy. Keep Dutch football terminology without over‑explaining: *wedstrijd, toernooi, zegereeks, achterstand, contributie, jeugdbeleidsplan, vrijwilligersbeleid, wedstrijdzaken, afgelastingen, blauw‑witte*.
- **No emoji.** No exclamation marks except in rare exceptional moments (e.g. a championship win).
- **"I" vs "you":** Mostly neither. The default narrator is **the club itself in third person**. Direct address (`je`, never `u`) is used for membership pages and recruitment, kept warm but plain.

### Vocabulary

| Do say | Don't say |
|---|---|
| DTS Ede blijft haar zegereeks voortzetten | We zijn trots om aan te kondigen dat onze innovatieve voetbalervaring… |
| WELKOM BIJ DTS EDE | Ons state‑of‑the‑art faciliteitencomplex biedt… |
| DTS Ede organiseert dit toernooi voor de 23e keer | Sluit je vandaag nog aan bij de DTS Ede familie en transformeer je voetbalreis! |
| Wedstrijd met twee gezichten | exclusive · premium · elite · luxury · cutting‑edge · revolutionary · disruptive · glamorous · prestigious · world‑class |

### One emoji exception (carefully)

The club's recreational/social senior team is named **`DTS Ede 6 🍺`** in the live navigation — the beer mug is part of the team's actual name. Carry that emoji through in any UI that lists the team. Outside that single named exception, the no‑emoji rule still holds.

## VISUAL FOUNDATIONS

### Colors

The palette is **two blues + a long gray ramp on near‑white** — and that is intentionally the whole story. No accents, no warm tones, no gradients in the brand book.

- **Royal Blue `#0060A0`** — primary. The crest, primary CTAs, link color, focused state. Used as a solid fill, never gradient.
- **Deep Blue `#0040A0`** — secondary. Hover/active darker variant of Royal Blue. Used for deep nav bars, score chips, jerseys when illustrated.
- **Off White `#F1F1F1`** — page surface, cards.
- **Light Gray `#DFDFDF`** — borders, dividers, disabled fields.
- **Silver Gray `#CCCCCC`** — secondary borders, table rules.
- **Medium Gray `#999999`** — captions, metadata, inactive icons.
- **Warm Gray `#888888`** · **Slate Gray `#666666`** · **Graphite `#555555`** — body text, secondary text.
- **Dark Charcoal `#333333`** · **Charcoal `#464646`** — primary body text on white.
- **Near Black `#222222`** — headlines.

The full ramp gives layout latitude without ever leaving the brand. **Never invent accent colors** (no green for "success", no red for "error") — use blue + gray + bold weight to express state. If a status indicator is unavoidable, treat it as a small pill in Royal Blue or a thin border, not a fill of a new hue.

### Type

- **Primary: HelveticaNeue** (Light / Roman / Medium / Bold / Black — full weight ladder in `fonts/`). Clean grotesque, unpretentious, prioritizes readability.
- **Additional / fallback:** Geneva, Droid Sans, Nexus, Roboto, Bitstream Charter — the brand book accepts these as fallbacks.
- **Scale** (brand‑book defined): 12 / 18 / 20 / 30 / 40 px (Small / Caption / Body / H3 / H2). We extend up to H1 = 64px and Display = 88px for hero use, holding the same family and weights.
- **Weight strategy:** body at **Light (300)** — exact match to the brand book. H3 at Medium (500). H1/H2 at Bold (700). Display tier (≥64px) at Black (900). Italics available in Bold and Black only — use sparingly, mostly for editorial quotes.
- **Tracking:** tight on display sizes; slightly open (`letter-spacing: 0.04em`) on UPPERCASE banners.

> ✅ **Full Helvetica Neue weight ladder licensed and loaded.** All five weights (300 / 400 / 500 / 700 / 900) ship with this design system via `@font-face` from `fonts/`. *Note: the file `HelveticaNeueRoman.otf` is the Linotype name for the 400/Regular weight — same glyphs, different label.*

### Spacing & layout

- A consistent **4px base unit**. Tokens: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96.
- **Layout is grid‑based and calm.** Single hero block at the top of a page (image + headline + short body), then a row or two of cards (news / fixtures / standings) on a 12‑column grid. Wide horizontal padding on desktop (`max-width: 1200px`, centered).
- **Cards do not float.** They are **flat rectangles** with a 1px Light Gray border and a `border-radius: 4px` — never glossy or floating.
- **Density is medium.** This is a club site, not a stat dashboard. Generous gaps, room to breathe.

### Backgrounds

- Page backgrounds: **Off White `#F1F1F1`** or pure white. No textures, no gradients, no patterns.
- **Hero banners may be full‑bleed photography** — authentic match action — overlaid with a translucent Royal Blue scrim (`rgba(0,96,160,0.55)`) when text sits over them, never a fashion‑photo gradient.
- The crest is not used as a watermark or background decoration.

### Imagery

- **Authentic action and community moments** — celebrations, huddles, training, volunteers, supporters. Outdoor, natural lighting.
- **60% action / 40% community moments.** Show all ages (U8 → 35+), both men's and women's teams.
- **No stock photos. No studio shots. No suits and handshakes.** No heavy filters or "professional sports" color grades.
- **Color vibe:** neutral / true‑to‑life. Slightly cool (Dutch outdoor light), never warm‑graded. Color, not black and white. Mild grain from real cameras is fine; no heavy film grain affectation.
- Where blue and white jerseys appear in‑frame they should be visible — they connect the imagery to the brand without typographic intervention.

### Borders, corners, shadows

- **Border radius: 4px** for cards and inputs, **2px** for chips and badges, **999px** (pill) only for the score chip on match cards.
- **Borders: 1px Light Gray `#DFDFDF`** is the default rule everywhere — table rows, card edges, form inputs.
- **Shadows are minimal.** Sticky headers may use a very faint elevation shadow (`0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)`). Cards do **not** get drop shadows — the border carries them.
- No glassmorphism, no inner shadows, no neumorphism.

### States

- **Hover:** primary buttons darken from Royal Blue → Deep Blue. Secondary buttons get a 4% Royal Blue tint on Off White. Links get an underline (no color change).
- **Press / active:** 2px translateY down with a slight darken — no shrink, no scale.
- **Focus:** a 2px Royal Blue outline at `outline-offset: 2px`. Keyboard focus is *visible* — this is a public‑facing club site.
- **Disabled:** Light Gray fill, Medium Gray text, no pointer events.

### Animation

- **Subtle and short.** 150–200ms ease‑out for hover/focus, 250ms for page transitions. No bounces, no spring physics, no parallax.
- The Easter Monday tournament page is allowed a slightly more celebratory moment (a single subtle banner reveal) but the rest of the site is quiet.

### Transparency, blur, gradients

- **Gradients: never** in UI chrome. The only acceptable gradient is the Royal Blue scrim over hero photography.
- **Blur: never** in cards or panels. Sticky headers are solid white.
- **Transparency:** only the photo scrim (`rgba(0,96,160,0.55)`), and a 50% black for image‑caption gradients on the very bottom of full‑bleed photos.

### Layout rules / fixed elements

- **Top nav is sticky**, white, with a thin Light Gray bottom border. Logo on the left, primary nav center, "Word lid" CTA on the right.
- **Footer is Deep Blue** with white text, four columns (Club / Voetbal / Toernooi / Contact). The crest sits top‑left at 48px.
- **Sponsor strip** — the live site carries a row of local sponsor logos (Autobedrijf Braber, Sportted, ING, Aannemersbedrijf W. Maassen, Sanidirect Ede, Concept4Cars, plus the *Club van 100* tile). They sit on a white band with even spacing, no decoration. Logos at their native colors, sized to share equal optical weight (max height ~56px).
- **Right rail widgets** on the homepage are small, plain, low‑chrome blocks: *Agenda*, *Onze blauw‑witte jarigen* (a tiny date+name table), *Zoek in deze site*, *Volg ons*, and the *Rookvrije generatie* badge linking to the smoke‑free park page. Each widget is just an H6 header in muted gray + content, no card chrome.
- **Rookvrij sportpark** — the round *Rookvrije generatie* mark is a recurring footer/sidebar element; treat it as a club‑level commitment marker, not decoration.

## ICONOGRAPHY

DTS Ede does not ship a proprietary icon set. The brand book recommends *"simple, bold iconography for wayfinding and section identification — prioritize clarity over decorative style."*

- **Icon library: Lucide** (`https://unpkg.com/lucide-static`). Stroke‑based, 24px grid, 1.5px stroke, rounded line caps. This matches the brand book's call for *clear, unpretentious, sober* iconography and pairs cleanly with the Helvetica‑style typography. **This is a substitution** — the brand book does not specify a library — and it's flagged: if you have a preferred icon set, swap the `lucide` references in the UI kit.
- **Color:** icons inherit `currentColor`. In nav: `#464646`. Active/hover: `#0060A0`. On dark backgrounds: white.
- **Size grid:** 16px (inline with body), 20px (nav), 24px (default), 32px (feature blocks).
- **Filled vs stroked:** **always stroked.** A filled icon is reserved for the single case of a play/pause control on the video player.
- **No emoji** anywhere in UI. The brand book is explicit about avoiding emoji and overly cartoonish styles. Unicode chars like `→` and `·` are fine in copy as a visual rhythm device.
- **The crest is not an icon.** It is the wordmark/logo, used at sizes ≥ 32px square and never as a list bullet.

### UI kits

- **`ui_kits/website`** — A recreation of dtsede.nl: sticky nav matching the real 7-section structure (Home · Onze club · Jeugd · Senioren · Wedstrijdzaken · Vrijwilligersbeleid · Contact), full‑bleed hero, latest news cards with **real headlines from the live site**, upcoming fixtures, league standings, sponsor strip, *blauw‑witte jarigen* sidebar widget, Deep Blue footer with real address and socials. **Note:** the live site is a Sportlink‑driven WordPress install whose visual chrome is plainer/older than this hi‑fi kit.
